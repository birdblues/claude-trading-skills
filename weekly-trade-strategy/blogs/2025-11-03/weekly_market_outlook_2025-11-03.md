## Executive Summary

The US stock market exhibits classic late-stage bull market characteristics with major indices at all-time highs (S&P 500: 6,836, Nasdaq: 25,827) but deteriorating internal market health. Breadth is narrowing dramatically with only 55-60% of stocks above their 200-day moving average, while large-cap technology continues driving gains. Multiple warning signals suggest elevated risk: extreme low Put/Call ratio (0.57), record margin debt ($1.13T, +38.5% YoY), compressed volatility (VIX: 17.43), and diverging market leadership as Russell 2000 declines while mega-caps rally. Market conditions warrant elevated caution despite bullish price action.
